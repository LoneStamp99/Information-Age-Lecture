The World Wide Web

WORLD WIDE WEB vs INTERNET
  Internet is a huge network of computers all connected together while the World Wide Web (www or ‘web’ for short) is a collection of webpages found on this network of computers. Very often, people think that the internet and the World Wide Web is same. However, these two things are different from each other.  
The World Wide Web gives users access to a vast array of documents that are connected to each other by means of hypertext or hypermedia links (hyperlinks). Hyperlinks are electronic connections that link related pieces of information in order to allow a user easy access to them.  It is the leading information retrieval service of the Internet. Web browsers uses the internet to access the web page.

                                                                    Tim Berners-Lee    

Origin of the World Wide Web

Historians trace the origin of the Internet to Claude E. Shannon , an American Mathematician who was considered as the “Father of Information Theory”. He worked at Bell Laboratories and at age 32, he published a paper proposing that information can be quantitatively encoded as a sequence of ones and zeroes. It was developed during 1970’s by the Department of Defense was used mainly by scientists to communicate with other scientists remained under government control until 1984. (Rouse, 2014) In 1989, the world wide web was invented by Sir Tim Berners-Lee and his colleagues at CERN (Conseil Européen pour la Recherche Nucléaire) or the European Organization for Nuclear Research) based in Geneva, Switzerland. He was trying to find a new way to easily share data from their experiments. Hypertext and internet already existed at this point but no one had thought of a way to use the internet to link once document directly to another.
                                                                    Tim Berners-Lee    

Development of World Wide Web

 1989. Sir Tim Berners-Lee wrote the original proposal for the world wide web. He wrote about the technologies needed to make the world wide web to work. These included the three main technologies (HTML, URL and HTTP) that were need and which remains in use today. (HTTPS, URL, And HTTP)
 1991. the first website went live at CERN in Europe Sir Tim Berners-Lee created the first-ever website. It was hosted by CERN in Switzerland, the research center he worked at. (Webserver & NeXT Computer) 
1992. The first web server outside of Europe was set up at Standford University in the USA.   There were only10 known web servers around the world in 1992. All the early web servers had been set up in Europe, this was the first in America.  
1993. CERN allowed anyone to use the web protocols and code for free.
The technologies that had been developed to make the world wide web became ‘open source’ so that anyone could use them for free.   People started to create websites for personal and online businesses.  The number of known servers increased to 50 during 1993.   § 1994. Telecommunications companies started to provide internet access. Access to the world wide web started to become available in people’s homes. The number of web servers increased to 623. During this year the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) as founded by Sir Tim Berners-Lee. The W3C is the organization that makes sure that the world wide web continues to develop.    

The importance of the Web

  The World Wide Web opened up the internet to everyone, not just scientists.  It
connected the world in a way that was not possible before and made it easier for people to get information, share and communicate. It allowed people to share their work and thoughts through social networking sites, blogs and video sharing. 

HOW TO CHECK RELIABILITY OF WEB SOURCES  
The internet contains a vast collection of highly valuable information but it may  also contain unrealiable, biased information that mislead people.  The following guidelines can help check the reliability of web sources being gathered. It is noteworthy to consider and apply these guidelines to avoid misinformation. (Lee College Library, n.d.) 
1.   WHO IS THE AUTHOR OF THE ARTICLE/SITE? § How to find out? Look for an “About” or “More About the Author” link at the top, bottom, or sidebar of the webpage. Some pages will have a corporate author rather than a single person as an author. If no information about the author(s) of the page is provided, be suspicious. •    Does the author provide his or her credentials? •    What type of expertise does he or she have on the subject he or she is writing about? Does he or she indicate what his or her education is? •    What type of experience does he or she have? Should you tryst his or her knowledge of the subject?       

The importance of the Web

2.  WHO PUBLISHED THE SITE? § How to find out? Look at the domain name of the website that will tell you who is hosting the site. For instance, the Lee College Library website is: http://www.lee.edu/library. The domain name is “lee.edu”. This tells you that the library website is hosted by Lee College. Search the domain name at http://www.whois.sc/. The site provides information about the owners registered domain names. What is the organization’s main purpose? Check the organization’s main website, if it has one. Is it educational? Commercial? Is it a reputable organization? Do not ignore the suffix on the domain name (the three-letter part that comes after the “.”).  The suffix is usually (but not always) descriptive of what type of entity hosts the website.   Keep in mind that it is possible for sites to obtain suffixes that are misleading. Here are some examples:
  •    .edu is for educational 
 •    .com is for commercial
  •    .mil is for military
  •    .gov is for government
  •    .org is for organization .    

The importance of the Web

3.  WHAT IS THE MAIN PURPOSE OF THE SITE? WHY DID THE AUTHOR WRITE IT AND WHY DID THE PUBLISHER POST IT?
  To sell a product? § As a personal hobby?  As public service? § To further scholarship on a topic?  To provide general information on a topic?  To persuade you of a particular point of view? 

  4.  WHO IS THE INTENDED AUDIENCE?  Scholars of the general public? § Which age group is it written for? § Is it aimed at members of a particular geographic area? § Is it aimed at members of a particular profession or with specific training?

   5. WHAT IS THE QUALITY OF INFORMATION PROVIDED ON THE WEBSITE?  Timeliness: When was the website first published? Is it regularly updated? 
Check for dates at the bottom of each page on the site.
 Does the author cite resources? Just as in print sources, web sources that cite their sources are considered more reliable.
 What type of other sites does the website link to?
Are they reputable sites?  What type of sites link to the website you are evaluating?
Is the website being cited by others?

Social Media

Top list Social Media

SOCIAL MEDIA

Social media is a term often used to refer to new forms of media that involve interactive participation. It may also refer to websites and applications that are designed to allow people to share content quickly, efficiently, and in real-time..

SOCIAL NETWORKING SITES 

The most traditional form of social media which allows users to interact with each other in different ways. Major platforms include Facebook, Twitter, and LinkedIn. Users can communicate with others through simple actions like tagging, hashtagging, commenting, and private messaging reacting to posts, and re-sharing content. One common feature is a newsfeed which is designed to show off a mic of text and visuals.

Interactive Media

This type of social media allows users to post about interesting niche topics, like memes, events, politics, and pop-culture. These platforms allow other users to share them or add to the conversation with their own commentary. Major Platforms include Tumblr and Reddit 

Photo Sharing

A type of social media specifically designed to share photos to other users. Major platforms include Instagram and Pinterest. 

Blogging/Community

This type of social media allows users to post about interesting niche topics, like memes, events, politics, and pop-culture. These platforms allow other users to share them or add to the conversation with their own commentary. Major Platforms include Tumblr and Reddit.

Facebook

A social networking site designed in 2004 by Harvard students Mark Zuckerberg, Eduardo Saverin, Andrew McCollum, Dustin Moskovitz and Chris Hughes originally intended to put college students in touch with one another, share their photos and meet new people.

Twitter

A microblogging system that allows a person to send and receive short posts called tweets that can be up to 140 characters long. It can include links to relevant websites and resources. It was founded in 2006 by Jack Dorsey along with Evan Williams, Biz Stone, and Noah Glass.

Linkiden

A social network founded in 2002 by Reid Hoffman, Allen Blue, Konstantin Guericke, Eric Ly and Jean-Luc Vaillant. It is specifically designed for career and business professionals to network; obtain resources and support; and build relationships with potential customers, clients, and partners.

Instagram

An American photo and video-sharing social networking service owned by Facebook, Inc. it was created by Kevin Systrom and Mike Krieger, and launched in October 2010.

Pinterest

A visual discovery tool that was originally created for people to share ideas and inspiration for various interests and projects. This social media site was founded by Ben Silbermann, Evan Sharp, and Paul Sciarra and was launched in 2010.

YouTube

A video sharing service that allows users to watch videos posted by other users and upload videos of their own. It was registered on February 14, 2005 by Steve Chen, Chad Hurley, and Jawed Karim, three former employees of the American e-commerce company PayPal.

Vimeo

A video sharing platform that was launched in 2004 by filmmakers Jade Lodwick and Zach Klein. It is very much known specifically for creative artistry.

Snapchat

A popular messaging app that lets users exchange pictures and videos called snaps that are meant to disappear after they’re viewed. Its essential function is to take a picture or video, add filters, lenses or other effects and share them. Founded in 2011 by Evan Spiegel, Reggie Brown, and Bobby Murphy, students at Stanford University.

TikTok

A Chinese short-form, video-sharing app that allows users to create and share a 15-second to 60-second videos, on any topic. It is owned by the Chinese company ByteDance founded in 2012 by Zhang Yiming. TikTok is formerly known as Musically. It was launched in 2017 for users outside China.

Tublr

A blogging platform for short-form microblogging, also known as tumblelogging. It is a social networking service that lets users create and post their own original content such as written entries, photographs, video clips or links to other websites. Founded by David Karp in 2007.

Riddet

A social news aggregation, web content rating, and discussion website. Members submit links, text posts, and images, which are then voted up or down by other members. Posts are organized by subject into user-created boards called “subreddits” which covers topics like news, science, movies, video games, music, books, fitness, food, and image-sharing. Founded by Steve Huffman and Alexis Ohanian in 2005.

Other forms of Social Media

OTHER FORMS OF SOCIAL MEDIA 

  EMAIL The most common form of social media used in everyday life. It involves users logging into an account in order to send and receive messages to other users.

  TEXTERS A two-way communicating channel that allows individuals to quickly send a message to another person or a group of people. Texters are derived from chatters, or computer programs that make use of the internet to allow people to quickly talk back and forth via text characters. 

 MESSAGE BOARDS An online discussion area in which users with similar interests discuss topics. These conversation or discussions are available in the form of posted messages. Members are allowed to view posts, post new queries or respond to existing queries posted by other members. This is also known as a forum, an online forum, and internet forum or a discussion board.
